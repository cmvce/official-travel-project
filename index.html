Why Lausanne?

Lausanne, The Olympic Capital

Lausanne is the capital and largest city of the Swiss French-speaking canton of Vaud.
Initially a Celtic and Roman settlement on the shores of the lake, Lausanne became a town at the foot of Notre Dame, a cathedral built in the 12th century. In the 20th century, Lausanne became a focus of international sport, hosting the International Olympic Committee (which has recognized the city as the "Olympic Capital" since 1994).

My favourite places in Lausanne

Downtown

At Dowtown, we strive to offer you a casual, fun and delicious experience in the heart of Lausanne. Our dishes are inspired by North American cuisines and prepared with local products. Whether it's for our famous brunches, for tapas with friends or for cocktail evenings, Downtown Lausanne is waiting for you!

Address:

Rte de Bel-Air 1, 1003 Lausanne

What I liked about it

Downtown is a fun café/restaurant. It's my go to if my wife and I don't know what to eat. The stuff is so polite and friendly! I'd suggest the 'Belphegor' dish for who like sweet and salty at same time.

La Mamma Osteria

La Mamma Osteria is an Italian Restaurant with classic pasta and pizza dishes. It's situated close to the train station, inside the Hotel "Alpha-Palmiers".

Address:

Rue du Petit-Chêne 34, 1003 Lausanne

What I liked about it

If you want pizza, this is the place to go to. Napolitan style pizza in Lausanne, it feels like you're in Naples again. I'd suggest the "Parma Bella". They also have good ol' steak.
